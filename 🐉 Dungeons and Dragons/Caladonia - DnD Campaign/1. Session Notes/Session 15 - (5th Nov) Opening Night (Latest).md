## 1. Strong Start
🎉 **Opening Night at The Gilded Gutter**
- The doors open for the first time — music swells, laughter fills the air.
- Describe warm lights, the scent of perfume and spirits, and the nervous pride of the party.
- Guests filter in: nobles, merchants, performers, familiar faces from Pennycross.
- NPCs mingle freely — each with a bit of flair and intrigue.
- The night feels like a success... until it doesn’t.

### 🎭 Opening Read-Aloud: "The Gilded Gutter Grand Opening"

The Gilded Gutter glows like a jewel in the heart of Port Reverie.

> Music drifts through its newly polished doors — laughter, clinking glass, the soft swell of strings tuning up on the main stage. The scent of roasted spice and sea breeze fills the air, carried on warmth from the kitchen where **Mirren**, your fiery chef, barks orders between bursts of laughter. Somewhere beyond the swinging doors, the goblin twins dart through the crowd with trays twice their size, arguing over who gets to serve the fancy nobles first.
> 
> Near the front, **Trevor the Troll** stands ramrod straight in a pressed vest and bowtie, looking both proud and faintly terrified as he checks invitations against a hand-scrawled list. His gravelly voice rumbles like distant thunder as he greets guests — polite, professional, and just a touch too loud. Behind him, his wife fusses over a toppled lantern, muttering about “the state of things when artists run a business.”
> 
> The chandeliers gleam. The brasswork shines. Every velvet curtain has been brushed to perfection. The Gilded Gutter has come alive — not just a cabaret, but a promise fulfilled.
> 
> A familiar sound cuts through the crowd: the sharp whistle of **Tweak**, the stagehand and tinkerer, perched up in the rafters adjusting the light rig. “Don’t start without me!” he calls, before vanishing into the maze of catwalks above.
> 
> You’ve built this place from ruin and rumor, and tonight, Port Reverie is watching.
> 
	Tonight, Port Reverie itself seems to have gathered under your roof. Dockhands fresh from the harbor rub shoulders with jewel-clad courtiers; masked envoys from the Silver Consortium sip gin beside swaggering privateers still smelling of salt and smoke. A pair of Azure League captains trade sharp jokes with a scholar from the university district, while a troupe of street performers heckles a merchant prince from the safety of the balcony rail. Everywhere, accents mingle, tempers flirt with civility, and the faint tension of a city balanced on its own ambition hums beneath the laughter.
> 
> *Welcome to the grand opening of the Gilded Gutter.*


🕰️ *After about 30–45 minutes of play*, as the festivities peak and the party relaxes — **Lady Seraphine Vance collapses mid-toast**, poisoned wine spilling across the floor.

Keep motives layered: love, legacy, and political ambition colliding under one roof.

## 🕯️ Additional Venue Scenes

---

### Scene 2A – A Toast in the Wine Bar
**Location:** The quiet, high-end corner where nobles congregate before the performance.  
**Tone:** Intimate, elegant, slightly pretentious.  

**Prompt:**  
> “A cluster of refined guests gathers in the wine bar, swirling glasses and murmuring about the ‘new blood’ running Port Reverie’s nightlife.”

**Interaction Hooks:**
- PCs overhear gossip about **Seraphine’s engagement** or the **DeVayne family’s debts**.  
- One noble mistakes a PC for staff and makes an **offensive comment** — will they swallow it or make a scene?  
- The bartender casually mentions the latest **Ironclad shipment of rare vintages**, quietly linking to the later poison.

**Rolls:** Persuasion, Insight, or Performance depending on approach.

---

### Scene 3B – Trouble in the Kitchen
**Location:** Mirren’s bustling kitchen.  
**Tone:** Tense, comedic, a flash of chaos.  

**Prompt:**  
> “Steam, clatter, and shouted orders. Mirren’s in her element — until she finds a missing ingredient, a broken cask, or an unexpected delivery from an unfamiliar supplier.”

**Interaction Hooks:**
- The suspicious **‘replacement cask’** could foreshadow the poison.  
- A kitchen assistant swears they saw **someone sneaking through the cellar hatch**.  
- PCs can help restore order, impress Mirren, or miss an early clue.

**Rolls:** Investigation or Insight; help actions can reduce later confusion when the murder hits.

---

### Scene 4A – The Performer’s Dressing Room
**Location:** Behind the stage; mirrors, makeup, perfumes.  
**Tone:** Intimate, nervous energy.  

**Prompt:**  
> “Perfume and powder fill the air. Performers whisper last-minute gossip as they ready for the show.”

**Interaction Hooks:**
- **Marilla** flirts, flatters, or fishes for secrets — she’s noticed Seraphine’s anxiety.  
- A performer accidentally mentions **‘extra bottles’** delivered for the private toast.  
- PCs might spot **Lila slipping through the back hall.**

**Rolls:** Persuasion or Insight to coax information; Perception to catch small details.

## 🕯️ Development Hook – Secrets of Lady Seraphine Vance

### Overview
The party can learn that Lady Seraphine’s life was far more complicated than her public grace suggested.  
Her friends and attendants reveal hints of **a forbidden romance, political manipulation, and quiet rebellion** against both her family and the Ironclad Company.

---

|------|---------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Marilla “The Velvet Voice” Vasquez** | Performer & confidante | Seraphine often came to the Gilded Gutter *alone* in recent weeks. She asked about “safe places” and secrecy spells. | Feels guilty — believes Seraphine planned to elope with someone not of her class. Will share a **folded note fragment** if persuaded (*“Meet me beneath the silver stage when the lights go out”*). |

| **Old Man Withers** | Long-time patron | Claims Seraphine met someone in the **smugglers’ tunnels** days earlier. Thinks it was a lover or messenger. | Rambling, half-drunk, but if pressed he remembers hearing **a woman cry** and **a hatch slam**. |

---

### 💡 Threads to Pull
- **The Broken Engagement:** The “arranged” marriage was an Ironclad power move; Seraphine wanted out.  
- **The Hidden Pendant:** A physical clue linking her to an Ironclad alchemist (same powder as the poison).  
- **The Masked Visitor:** The assassin used the tunnels; the glove corroborates this.  
- **The Shipment:** The poison traces back to the Ironclad Company’s imported vintages.  

---

### 🧩 Story Impact
Discovering these connections reveals that Seraphine’s death wasn’t just a crime of passion — it was **a political assassination disguised as a family tragedy.**  
Depending on who the PCs trust or accuse:
- They can expose Ironclad’s orchestration (earning powerful enemies).  
- Frame the DeVaynes to maintain peace.  
- Or hide the truth and gain leverage with Captain Dorne.
