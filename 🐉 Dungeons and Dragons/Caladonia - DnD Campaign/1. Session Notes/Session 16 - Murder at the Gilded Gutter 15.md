
## Session Recap

The night began in celebration.

The Gilded Gutter opened its doors for the first time, and Port Reverie answered. Music filled the room, glasses clinked, and the bar thrumed with the energy of something new — something people wanted to believe in.

Dockhands and merchants mixed with nobles and performers, rivalries hidden behind polite smiles. The Gilded Gutter wasn’t just open — it was being judged.

Baron moved through the crowd with intent, making herself visible, composed, and impressive among the wealthier guests. Eyes lingered. Conversations paused. This place — and the people behind it — mattered.

Lady Seraphine Vance stood out immediately: well-dressed, gracious, and at ease, laughing easily as attention gathered around her.

As the night deepened, talk turned to indulgence. Rare perfumes. Stimulants. Excess. During one such exchange, Banjolina quietly lifted a small vial from a perfumer named Renn — and drank it. Whatever the consequences, they did not come immediately.

And the night was going well.

Zhik’ta and Captain Penelope ran the bar flawlessly, serving drinks with confidence and flair. Compliments flowed. In the kitchen, Mirren’s food emptied plates and won quiet praise — even the infamous “toilet hobo wine” became a talking point rather than a joke.

For a brief, shining stretch, the Gilded Gutter wasn’t just open. It was _working_.

Then Lady Seraphine raised her glass — and collapsed mid-toast. Wine shattered on the floor. The music stopped. Panic rippled through the room.

You barred the doors and sent for the Salt Watch. Captain Alaric arrived soon after and placed the venue under lockdown. Statements were taken. The room fell quiet, heavy with suspicion.

In the confusion, DR-55 noticed a halfling trying to slip away. She called herself Lila — a thief, not a killer. A quiet deal was struck, and she vanished through the old smuggling tunnels beneath the cellar.

When the night finally stilled, Lady Seraphine lay stricken, the Watch held the doors, and the Gilded Gutter’s grand opening had become the scene of a crime.

The celebration was over.

## 🎬 New Session Opening (Post–Time Skip)

### Strong Start: _The Summons_

Open with a **new inciting incident**, not the old one.

**Read-Aloud**

> “The Gilded Gutter is open again — technically.
> > The lanterns burn lower now. Conversations stop when uniforms pass by.
> > Three days have gone by since Lady Seraphine collapsed on your floor.
> > This morning, a sealed summons arrived.
> > Captain Alaric of the Salt Watch requests your presence — together — at the Field Office.
> > It is not optional.”

This immediately:
- acknowledges the past
- moves the story forward
- gives the players a _clear reason to act_

---

## 🧭 What the Players Are Dealing With _Now_

Re-anchor the session around **current pressures**, not old clues.
### The New Status Quo
- The Gilded Gutter has a **reputation**
- The Watch believes this was **targeted**, but unresolved
- Nobles are cautious; rivals are circling
- Someone is unhappy the investigation stalled
- Someone else is relieved it did

This is where play resumes.

---

## 🧩 The New Central Question

Not _“Who poisoned Seraphine?”_  
But:

> **“What are you going to do about the truth you _might_ uncover?”**

## 🧱 Session Structure (Lazy DM, Time-Skip Friendly)

### 1️⃣ Scene One – The Official Version

**Location:** Salt Watch Field Office
Captain Alaric presents:
- the **official findings**
- what the Watch _believes_
- what they are prepared to act on

**Key Reveal (choose one):**
- The Watch has a **suspect**, but weak evidence
- The Watch has **no suspect**, but political pressure
- The Watch believes the poison came from _outside_ the bar

Alaric then asks:

***The Salt Watch field office smells of oil, ink, and wet wool.***  
***A city map is pinned to the wall, marked with chalk circles and notes you don’t recognize.***  
***Lady Seraphine’s name appears once — underlined.***

***Captain Alaric doesn’t rise when you enter.***  
***He studies a page for a long moment, then finally looks up at you.***

***“Three days ago, a noblewoman was poisoned during a public gathering in your establishment.***

***Since then, my people have taken statements, examined samples, chased rumors, and listened to a great deal of very expensive outrage.”***

***He taps the page in front of him.***

***“Here is what we know.”***

***“Lady Seraphine Vance was killed — not by accident, not by impulse. The poison was refined, measured, and administered by someone who expected to walk away.”***

***“It was not random. It was not a street crime. And it was not meant to start a riot.”***

***He folds his hands.***

***“Which means someone believed the aftermath could be managed.”***


This reframes cooperation as _a new choice_, not a continuation.

---

### 2️⃣ Scene Two – Consequences at the Gilded Gutter

Back at the bar:

- fewer patrons
    
- heavier glances
    
- sharper rumors
    

Give each PC **one visible consequence**:

- Baron is treated differently by nobles
    
- DR-55 notices Watch attention linger
    
- Staff are nervous; Mirren is angry
    
- A rival tavern offers “help”
    

This restores emotional stakes _without memory reliance_.

---

### 3️⃣ Scene Three – The Delayed Hook Pays Off

**Lila finally reappears.**

Not sneakily — **intentionally**.

Options:

- A note delivered to the bar
    
- A meeting request in a neutral location
    
- Lila already in trouble when they find her
    

What matters:

- She waited on purpose
    
- Things changed while she was gone
    
- She now knows **who is dangerous**
    

Her information is no longer “what happened” but:

- who benefited
    
- who paid
    
- who is trying to bury it
    

---

## 🧭 The New Investigation (Forward-Facing)

Instead of retracing the crime, players now investigate:

### A) **Who Interfered After the Poisoning**

- Evidence lost
    
- Statements altered
    
- Pressure applied
    

### B) **Who Is Threatened by the Truth**

- Noble houses
    
- Trade interests
    
- Criminal intermediaries
    

### C) **What Lila Walked Into**

- Someone followed her
    
- Someone else wanted her silenced
    
- Someone assumed she’d talk
    

This makes the mystery _active again_.

---

## 🔀 Branching Paths (Post-Time Skip)

### 🛡️ Path 1: Work With the Watch (Again)

- Expose interference
    
- Gain protection
    
- Make powerful enemies
    

### 🗝️ Path 2: Keep It Quiet

- Leverage secrets
    
- Protect the bar
    
- Become complicit
    

### 🔥 Path 3: Take It Public

- Leak information
    
- Force action
    
- Burn social capital
    

Each path **moves the campaign forward**, not sideways.

---

## 🧠 Lazy DM Principles (Why This Works)

- No recall required
    
- Past events are summarized as _facts_
    
- New agency comes from **new choices**
    
- The mystery evolves instead of resets
    
- The time gap feels _intentional_, not awkward
    

You’re telling the table:

> “That night mattered — and now we see how.”
