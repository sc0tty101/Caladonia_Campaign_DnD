### 📍 Where We Are

The party owns and operates **the Gilded Gutter**, a cabaret venue in the **Pennycross** district of Port Reverie. On opening night, a prominent noblewoman — **Lady Seraphine Vance** — was poisoned in front of everyone and the Salt Watch locked down the venue.

Rather than be implicated, the party cut a deal with **Captain Alaric** of the Salt Watch: he buys them time, they investigate in places he can't go.

After several sessions of legwork they have effectively **solved the murder.** They just need to prove it.

---

### 🔍 What the Party Knows

- The poison used was **Midnight Bellflower** — extremely rare and expensive, pointing to a wealthy buyer
- The sales ledger from the **Rose Glass dispensary** in Silkwater contains the initials **AW**
- **Lord Aurelian Westcott** is the obvious connection — he was Lady Seraphine's very public suitor, and the two had a loud, ugly argument outside the **Whispering Cup** three nights before the murder
- Rumours are swirling that Seraphine broke off the relationship and was planning to expose the Westcott family's financial ruin
- A halfling thief named **Lila** — spotted trying to slip away on opening night by DR-55 — noted that Seraphine's personal attendant was acting flustered and out of sorts that evening, unusual for someone known to be calm

---

### 🔑 What They Don't Know Yet (But Are Close To)

- The poison was actually administered by **Elowen Thrice**, Seraphine's long-time personal attendant, under duress
- Westcott is holding Elowen's family hostage to ensure her silence
- The Westcott family is secretly on the verge of complete financial ruin — the marriage to Seraphine was their lifeline
- Elowen will talk if her family's safety can be guaranteed

---

### ▶️ Where to Pick Up

The party's clearest next move is the **Whispering Cup** — the site of the public argument and their best lead on what happened between Westcott and Seraphine. Head there, find **Pell** (a regular patron who witnessed the aftermath of the argument), and Elowen's name surfaces naturally. From there it's the warehouse rescue and then Westcott.

---

### 🎯 Tips for Getting the Table Warmed Up

**Open with a quick "previously on."** Don't assume everyone remembers the details after a few weeks away. Give the party a genuine two-minute recap at the table before you start — frame it like a TV show cold open. It's not embarrassing, it's good DMing.

**Put something in their hands immediately.** As they arrive at the Whispering Cup, Westcott's two retainers are already inside watching the door. That visual — _he knows we're asking questions_ — instantly re-establishes stakes and gets everyone leaning forward.

**Let them talk to each other first.** After a holiday break the table needs a few minutes to reconnect. Don't rush the first scene. A bit of banter over breakfast at the Gilded Gutter before they head out is completely fine and gets everyone back in character naturally.

**Don't re-explain everything you know.** Give out clues generously this session — the party has earned the right to feel smart and make quick connections. This is the payoff session, not a new mystery.

## 🎭 Session 19 Prep — _The Westcott Affair_



---

### 🗺️ The Strong Start

Start _in media res_ — the party is already moving. They've had the night to think, they have AW's initials from the ledger, and they know the Whispering Cup is where the public argument happened. Open with:

_"It's early morning in Silkwater. The canals are quiet, the gondoliers are just starting their rounds, and the Whispering Cup's unmarked bronze door is already open for its first patrons."_

No nudge needed. They did the legwork. They're here because it's the obvious next move — and the city is starting to feel the pressure of what they've set in motion.

---

### 🎯 Scene Outline

#### Scene 1 — The Whispering Cup

What they're looking for is a witness to what happened _after_ the argument — specifically the moment Westcott left and Elowen stayed behind with Seraphine. That witness is already in the room: **Pell**, a quietly observant gnome clerk who comes every Tuesday and sits in the same alcove. He noticed everything because he always does, and he has no particular reason to stay quiet. He just needs to be asked the right question.

**What Pell can confirm:** After Westcott stormed off, Seraphine stayed and had a long, intense conversation with her attendant — a calm, well-dressed woman. Seraphine did most of the talking. The attendant left first, visibly distressed. Seraphine sat alone for another hour before leaving.

That attendant was Elowen Thrice. The party now has her name, her role, and a clear reason to find her.

**The tension in the room:** Two of Westcott's household retainers are already seated near the entrance — not drinking, just watching. They were here before the party arrived. The implicit message is clear: Westcott knows the Cup is a loose end and he's trying to manage it.

This is a social tension scene with multiple approaches:

- **Charm:** Getting Pell talking is easy — he's not guarded, just overlooked. A warm approach and a bought drink is enough. The retainers are harder but not immovable — pointing out that their presence here is itself a form of taking sides can rattle them.
- **Fight:** The retainers are under orders to intimidate, not kill. If things escalate they'll try to flee, turning this into a **chase through Silkwater's canal district** — gondolas, wet cobblestones, narrow bridges. Thunderwave into a canal is an acceptable and encouraged outcome.
- **Bard flex:** A well-placed Performance check to draw the room's attention lets someone slip into conversation with Pell unobserved while the retainers are distracted. Vicious Mockery used to make the retainers argue with each other is also completely valid.

---

#### Scene 2 — Finding Elowen

Elowen is the emotional centrepiece of the session. She's been quietly confined to Manor Vance since the murder, ostensibly grief-stricken and not receiving visitors. Westcott has made it known she is not to be disturbed.

**Getting to her:**

- **Snaggletooth and the goblin kids** are perfect here. They can slip through the servants' entrance without raising flags and pass a note. Reward the party for thinking to use their own people.
- **Lila the halfling thief** owes DR-55 a favour and knows the servant networks of Silkwater cold. She can get a message inside for the debt repaid.
- A **bold front door approach** works too — the Vance household staff are frightened and quietly desperate for someone to help them. A credible bluff as Salt Watch representatives gets the party through the door.

**The scene with Elowen** is where you slow down and let it breathe. She's not coy or evasive — she's exhausted, hollowed out by guilt, and barely holding together. She loved Lady Seraphine. She administered the poison because Westcott gave her no choice — he is holding her family and has made the consequences of refusal very clear.

She'll crack quickly if the party offers her any genuine compassion or a credible promise of protection. She tells them everything:

- What Westcott threatened and when
- That the poison came from the Rose Glass
- Where her family is being held — a Westcott-owned bonded goods warehouse in Crown Harbor
- That she will testify publicly if her family is safe

This is a Persuasion scene but make it feel human, not mechanical. She's not a locked door — she's a person who needs someone to tell her it's going to be okay.

---

#### Scene 3 — The Warehouse

This is your action beat and the session's excitement pivot. A bonded goods warehouse on the Crown Harbor docks, Westcott-owned, active enough at night that dock workers and shipments provide cover.

**Layout (simple):**

- Ground floor: open space, crates and barrels, two guards playing cards with their crossbows on the table rather than in hand
- Upper mezzanine: two more guards with line of sight down to the floor below, stairs on one side
- Back room: small, walled off, locked with a mundane padlock — Elowen's family inside, unlit

**The Foreman:** A nervous human who is very much not a villain. He has a family of his own and took Westcott's coin before he understood what he was being paid to do. He will actively help the party if given any face-saving reason to cooperate.

**Bard-adapted encounter tips:**

_If they fight:_ The guards are hired mercenaries, not soldiers. If two go down the remaining two make a DC 13 Wisdom save or disengage and flee. The foreman does not fight under any circumstances. Mid-combat Persuasion to get guards to stand down is valid and should be rewarded — these men are not dying for Westcott's debts.

_If they charm:_ A credible bluff that the Salt Watch is right behind them will immediately buckle the foreman. Westcott's men are more disciplined but a Suggestion sending one away on a fake errand, or Vicious Mockery causing guards to fumble and argue with each other, should visibly rattle the rest. The party should feel their bardic toolkit working.

_If they're sneaky:_ A shipment is being moved tonight. Dock workers are coming and going, there's noise and lantern light and plenty of cover. The two downstairs guards have their crossbows on the table — the party gets a free round if they're careful.

**The twist:** As the party frees the hostages, one retainer slips out and rides hard for Westcott. The clock is now ticking — Westcott knows his plan is unraveling and will start making desperate decisions.

---

#### Scene 4 — The Confrontation

Lord Aurelian Westcott is not a fighter. He's a desperate, cornered nobleman who genuinely believed he had no other option. When the party finds him — at his manor, or fleeing toward the docks — this should feel like the end of a thriller, not a dungeon encounter.

He'll move through three stages if pressed:

- _Confident:_ "You have nothing. Initials in a ledger? A frightened attendant's word? My lawyers will have this dismissed before dawn."
- _Rattled:_ "She was going to ruin us. Everything my family built — gone, because she found out we borrowed money?"
- _Broken:_ "I didn't think it would go this far. I just needed more time."

**For a bard party the most satisfying ending is a public unmasking, not a fight.** If the party is clever they manoeuvre Westcott into confessing somewhere with witnesses. The Whispering Cup's enchanted alcoves cut both ways — they can project a confession as easily as they muffle one, if someone positions themselves correctly. That's a spectacular, bard-worthy ending that rewards the whole investigation.

Captain Alaric makes the arrest — but only if the party delivers the evidence cleanly and publicly enough that Westcott's lawyers can't buy him out of it.

---

### 🌆 Making the City Feel Alive

Weave these in as background texture throughout — not plot, just the city breathing and changing around the party:

**The Gilded Gutter's reputation is shifting.** The poisoning at opening night is all over Pennycross gossip. Half the district thinks the place is cursed or involved. The other half thinks it's the most exciting thing that's happened in years. Miss Pella has been telling anyone who'll listen that she _always_ knew something dramatic would happen there. Snaggletooth's goblin kids are already hawking "I was there when Lady Seraphine was poisoned" stories for a copper a piece.

**The Salt Watch is under pressure.** Alaric is visibly stressed. A Westcott family lawyer has filed a formal complaint with the Wheel of Nine suggesting the investigation is politically motivated. The party has roughly three days before it gets shut down from above.

**Aetherium murmurs are escalating.** Crude woodblock-printed posters have appeared around Pennycross — a glowing metal ingot with the words _"They're selling the future. Ask who's buying."_ The Marking House Consortium is dispatching inspectors to tear them down. The Workers' Compact is quietly not stopping them.

**The Velvet Knives make their presence felt.** Harry and Harold have sent a handwritten note to the Gilded Gutter on very fine paper with a pressed flower. _"We heard about your opening. Such a pity about the unfortunate guest. We do hope you'll let us make it up to you — dinner, perhaps? We've been so eager to meet the new proprietors."_ No urgency. Just dread.

### 💡 Sly Flourish Essentials

**Secrets and clues — give these out generously, don't hoard them:**

- Westcott's retainers have been seen near the Gilded Gutter twice since the poisoning
- The Westcott family fortune is a fiction — three banks have quietly declined to extend further credit
- Elowen has a younger brother who works at the Quiet Quays fish market, easy to find if the party looks
- A gondolier in Silkwater saw a man matching Westcott's description leaving Manor Vance the night of the murder, not arriving
- The Whispering Cup keeps a ledger of favours owed — the Westcotts have outstanding debts with Maerrow

**Fantastic locations:**

- The Whispering Cup — tension without violence, power without open threat
- The canal chase through Silkwater at night if the retainers bolt — torchlit bridges, gondolas, wet cobblestones
- The warehouse — industrial, shadowy, full of crates and moral ambiguity
- Wherever Westcott finally cracks — make it feel earned

**Three NPCs to prep, just a few beats each:**

- **Pell** — gnome clerk, observant, no agenda, just needs to be asked
- **Elowen Thrice** — exhausted, guilty, loves Seraphine, wants this to be over
- **Lord Aurelian Westcott** — charming when confident, brittle when cornered, genuinely believes he had no choice

---

### ⚡ Escalation Options

If the session is dragging, use any of these:

- Lila shows up at the Gutter breathless — Westcott's men are asking about the party down at the docks and looking at their ship
- A Salt Watch inspector (not Alaric) arrives and asks the party to come in for formal questioning — someone reported the ledger theft at the Rose Glass
- Snaggletooth bursts in: Elowen has been moved — Westcott is getting nervous and covering his tracks

---

### 🎬 Potential Session Ending Beats

**Best case:** Westcott is publicly exposed, Alaric makes the arrest, and the party is cleared. The Gilded Gutter gets a broadsheet write-up: _"Proprietors of Pennycross's Newest Cabaret Unmask Noble Murderer."_ Business booms.

**Cliffhanger:** Westcott is arrested but his lawyers file an injunction. He's not in a cell yet — he's in Ninefold Hall arguing his case. The party has one more night to make it stick.

**Wild card:** Rather than be ruined, Westcott flees — and the party has to decide whether to chase him or let Alaric handle it. His flight takes him toward the docks. Toward a ship flying a very familiar flag.
