# 🎭 Session 18 Prep — The Rose Glass Job

## Session 18 - Session Notes
The party spend some more time trying to figure out how to get access to the sales details in order to find some clues as to who purchased the poison. After a failed attempt of deceit by Banjolina, DR-55 returns to the apocathy as Sir Mixalot and collects his order from the previous day. He skillfully manages to steal the sales log and escape without being noticed. 

Within the logbook - are the initials AW - who the party can connect as Aurelion Westcott. Lord Westcott is a wealthy and powerful figure in the city. More importantly, Aurelion and Lady Seraphine had been a high profile couple of Port Reverie - bringing together two of the wealthiest families (the Westcotts and the Vances). Nothing was official - however the two stopped being seen together and rumors were swirling that Lady Seraphine had opted to continue to search for other eligible bachelors.

The party return to Cpt Alaric and share what they have found. Alaric is alarmed - the Westcotts are very powerful so the situation must be handled carefully - but Alaric is willing to put his neck out for the group if they can bring proof that Westcott was behind the murder.

**Current leads + focuses for the party:**
* What happened between Lord Westcott and Lady Seraphine - did their relationship sour?
* How can you prove that Lord Westcott was at the Gilded Gutter that night? He wasn't known to you at the time but none of you noticed him at the event that evening.
* The Westcotts are powerful and will fight this tooth and nail. What other information or tactics are needed in order for Alaric to bring the full weight of the Saltwatch and bring justice to Lady Seraphine and clear your name.

## The Strong Start

Open _in media res_ at Bistro Bennito's. The party are finishing their surveillance, plates cleared, wine half-drunk, watching the Rose Glass through the window as the afternoon light fades into evening. The guard rotation is now familiar. Madame Lysenne's light is still on upstairs.

> _"The ledger is in there. Lysenne knows who bought the Midnight Bellflower. Between you and the truth: one locked door, one very composed woman, and whatever she keeps in that back room."_

Drop them straight into the planning/execution. No preamble needed — they know the goal.

**Complications to have ready** (use one, not all):

## Key NPCs

**Madame Lysenne Valeur** — Pale gloves, glass hairpins, rosewater-and-antiseptic scent. Measured and untouchable. She is not cruel, just entirely transactional. She values the _fiction_ of legitimacy more than the reality. Attack that, and she bends. Attack her directly, and she becomes an enemy with very long reach.

**Elowen Thrice** — The party doesn't know it yet, but she's cracking. Her family is still being held. She was at the opening night. She _wants_ to be found. If anyone goes to Manor Vance or asks around Silkwater about Seraphine's circle, Elowen may surface — jumpy, pale, watching exits.

**Lord Aurelian Westcott** — Not present tonight, but he's aware the investigation is moving. He has eyes in Silkwater. If the party has been visible (and they have — Bistro Bennito's all afternoon), he may already know.

## Fantastic Locations

### Bistro Bennito's _(current position)_

Warm candlelight, checkered tablecloths, the smell of garlic butter and cheap Silkwater wine. The kind of place that's just respectable enough to not raise eyebrows, but just busy enough to disappear into. The owner — a sweaty, cheerful human named Bennito — refills glasses without being asked and gossips freely if you seem like you're staying a while.

_Use it as:_ A planning hub, a place to regroup mid-session, or where Therrum Mountaintale inconveniently appears.

---

### The Rose Glass Dispensary _(upper promenade)_

White stone frontage, frosted glass windows etched with botanical motifs. Inside: the sweet-sour smell beneath the rosewater, shelves arranged with surgical precision, and Madame Lysenne behind the counter — pale gloves, glass hairpins, and a ledger sitting in plain sight because she's never needed to hide it before. The soft silver door chime announces every arrival. After hours, the amber glow from upstairs suggests she lives above the shop.

_Use it as:_ The primary heist target. The ledger is the prize. The locked back door to the Rose Thorn below is a secondary mystery.

---

### The Rose Thorn _(below the dispensary)_

If they find the hidden dispensary beneath: stone steps, cold air, shelves of things that shouldn't be available over a counter. Vials with no labels. Orders waiting for collection with only initials on the tags. The Velvet Knives have been here. So has someone with the initials A.W.

_Use it as:_ A deeper revelation — the Rose Glass's legitimate face hides something considerably darker underneath.

---

### The Shrine of the Tidemother _(Silkwater, canal-side)_

A small open-air shrine tucked between two townhouses where the Azure Canal widens into a small reflecting pool. Votive candles float on the water in tiny paper boats. Seraphine visited here the day she died. A priestess named **Ysolde** tends it most evenings — quiet, observant, and bound by oath not to repeat confessions, but not bound from confirming whether someone visited.

Fresh flowers have been left at the shrine since the murder. Someone is still coming.

_Use it as:_ A clue location — Elowen may have come here too. Ysolde noticed something but won't say what without careful handling.

---

### The Whispering Cup _(Silkwater, upper promenade)_

The unmarked bronze door. The doorman who doesn't ask names. Sound-dampening alcoves, crystal decanters that refuse to pour for the unworthy, and **Maerrow Ilthane** behind it all — impeccably polite, unfailingly neutral, and far more informed than he lets on. The party may not get in easily, but the staff remember the argument between Westcott and Vance three nights before the murder.

If they can get a seat, getting Maerrow to confirm the argument is straightforward — he _wants_ it known, carefully, to the right people. It suits him. What it costs them is another matter.

_Use it as:_ Confirmation of the Westcott-Vance falling out, plus a taste of a location they'll want to return to repeatedly.

---

### Manor Vance _(Silkwater, hillside)_

Seraphine's home, now quiet and watched by two Salt Watch officers at the gate who are very much _not_ under Alaric's command. The household staff are frightened and grieving. **Elowen Thrice** has not left the manor since the night of the murder. She is pale, barely sleeping, watching every door. She's not hiding. She's waiting to be caught, because she has no other way out.

_Use it as:_ The emotional heart of the investigation. Elowen is the confession waiting to happen — but she won't talk until she believes her family is safe.

---

### The Lords Cask _(Silkwater, lower promenade)_

A wine merchant's storefront that is significantly more interesting inside than its modest frontage suggests. **Maelin Corrow** is a nervous, meticulous half-elf who keeps obsessive records of every bottle sold, to whom, and when. He supplied the wine brought to the Gilded Gutter that evening — and he is _very_ eager to establish that he had nothing to do with the poison.

He'll talk. Possibly too much. His records show the wine was ordered three days prior, collected in person by a woman matching Elowen's description, and paid for in cash — unusual for an order of that quality.

_Use it as:_ A clean, low-risk lead that confirms Elowen handled the wine and gives the party a clear timeline.

---

### The Silkwater Watchhouse _(bridge approach)_

Captain Jameson runs a tight, well-funded post staffed by guards considerably better dressed than their Pennycross counterparts. Secretary Silvana is back at her desk. If the party sweet-talked their way past last time, they're on borrowed time before someone connects the descriptions. Westcott has a social relationship with Jameson — not corrupt exactly, but they attend the same club. Jameson wouldn't bury a murder, but he would ask politely that certain _methods_ of investigation be conducted elsewhere.

_Use it as:_ Pressure. The party is not invisible in Silkwater and someone well-connected is paying attention.

---

### The Azure Canal at Night

After dark the gondoliers stop running and the canal reflects the floating crystal lights in long, trembling lines across the water. Quiet enough to hear a conversation from the opposite bank. **Rosa Waterwise**, the gondolier captain, doesn't officially work nights — but for the right coin she makes exceptions. She knows every discreet landing point, servants' dock, and unlocked garden gate along the canal. She also saw Westcott's coach outside Manor Vance two nights ago, which she thought was odd given what everyone's heard.

_Use it as:_ A transit option, an eavesdropping opportunity, or a way to move around Silkwater without being seen on the main streets.

### Lord Aurelian Westcott

_The Architect of the Murder_

**Appearance:** Late 40s, silver-templed, impeccably dressed. The kind of man who looks expensive even when he's trying to seem casual. Carries a silver-topped cane he doesn't need.

**Voice & Manner:** Warm, measured, and faintly condescending. Speaks like he's doing you a favour by talking to you. Never raises his voice. Laughs at things that aren't funny.

**What he wants right now:** To know how close the investigation is. To appear unbothered. To find out who is asking questions about the Vance case without appearing to care.

**What he's afraid of:** That Elowen breaks. That the ledger at the Rose Glass gets found. That the debt becomes public knowledge before he can control the narrative.

**3 things to say as Westcott:**

- _"Lady Vance was a dear friend. Her loss has been... considerable."_ (pause, move on quickly)
- _"I wasn't aware the Gilded Gutter was in the business of criminal investigation. How delightfully ambitious."_
- _"I'd be careful about the company you keep in Silkwater. People here have long memories and short patience for intrusion."_

**If cornered or accused:** He doesn't get angry — he gets very still and very polite, and suddenly every word feels like a threat wrapped in silk. He will not break in a confrontation. He will smile, excuse himself, and immediately send someone to deal with the problem.

### Elowen Thrice

_Seraphine's Attendant — The Reluctant Killer_

**Appearance:** Mid-30s human woman. Usually composed and elegant — that's her whole identity, the calm one in the room. Right now her eyes are red-rimmed, her hands won't quite stay still, and she's lost weight in a week.

**Voice & Manner:** Speaks carefully and quietly, like she's choosing every word to avoid saying the wrong one. Keeps glancing at doors. Flinches at loud sounds.

**What she wants right now:** For someone to find her family. For someone to tell her it's going to be alright. To confess to literally anyone — the weight of it is destroying her.

**What she's afraid of:** That Westcott finds out she's been found. That her family dies anyway. That she'll hang for something she was forced to do.

**3 things to say as Elowen:**

- _"I can't— I don't know what you're talking about."_ (said while clearly knowing exactly what they're talking about)
- _"She was my friend. She was my— "_ (doesn't finish the sentence)
- _"If I tell you anything, he'll know. He always knows."_

**The crack:** She doesn't need much. If the party shows genuine compassion rather than accusation, or if they can credibly promise her family's safety, she falls apart almost immediately. She's not protecting Westcott — she's protecting her family. Separate those two things and she tells them everything.

**If pushed too hard:** She shuts down completely and won't speak. She needs to feel safe, not cornered.

### Madame Lysenne Valeur

_Apothecary — Knows Everything, Says Nothing_

**Appearance:** Immaculate woman in her 40s. Pale gloves always. Hair pinned with alchemical glass needles. Faint smell of rosewater over something sharper underneath.

**Voice & Manner:** Never rushes. Never raises her voice. Treats every interaction like a transaction she's already calculated the outcome of. Remembers everything.

**What she wants right now:** For these people to leave her shop without incident and never come back.

**What she's afraid of:** Her reputation. Specifically: being publicly associated with the Vance murder. She sold the poison legally — to her — but legal doesn't mean safe right now.

**3 things to say as Lysenne:**

- _"I'm afraid I don't discuss my clients. You understand."_
- _"Therrum Mountaintale speaks highly of the Gilded Gutter. He's never mentioned you personally."_ (said with a smile that means she knows the bluff)
- _"I keep very thorough records. For my protection, you understand. Not yours."_

**The lever:** Her reputation is everything. A credible, specific threat to expose her to the wrong people — not violence, not blackmail — just the right name hearing the wrong thing — is the only thing that moves her. She may offer one piece of unsolicited information in exchange for silence. Let that be something useful but dangerous.

### Maerrow Ilthane

_Proprietor of the Whispering Cup — Port Reverie's Most Informed Neutral Party_

**Appearance:** Ageless, elegant, always in grey. Moves through his own establishment like a ghost who owns the place — because he does.

**Voice & Manner:** Soft-spoken. Says half of what he means and means all of it. Phrases things as observations rather than statements. Never lies — just omits strategically.

**3 things to say as Maerrow:**

- _"Lord Westcott and Lady Vance were regular guests. The evening you're referring to was... notable."_
- _"I don't trade in gossip. I trade in context. There's a difference."_
- _"You may find the information you're looking for. Whether you're ready for where it leads is another matter entirely."_

---

### Maelin Corrow

_Wine Merchant — Nervous, Talkative, Innocent_

**Appearance:** Thin half-elf, late 40s, ink-stained fingers, always slightly sweaty. Keeps his ledgers immaculate because controlling his records is the one thing he can control.

**Voice & Manner:** Talks too fast when nervous, which is always. Volunteers information unprompted because silence makes him more anxious than speaking.

**3 things to say as Maelin:**

- _"I had nothing to do with — I mean, I sold the wine, yes, but selling wine is legal, obviously, that's my entire business—"_
- _"She paid in cash. I thought it was unusual. I wrote it down. I write everything down."_
- _"A woman. Well-dressed. Composed. Collected it herself. Three days before — before."_
