### **Full details of the Gilded Gutter that the party came up with.**
- Cabaret-style headquarters in Port Reverie combining business, residence, and covert operations under one roof.
- Main cabaret hall with elevated stage, velvet curtains, warm lighting, ornate décor, and tiered dining tables; used for performances, galas, faction gatherings, and major story events.
- Side bar lounge functions as an always-open tavern space, allowing casual drinking, tapas service, downtime roleplay, and quieter intrigue even when the stage is inactive.
- Separate upscale wine bar provides an intimate corner for wealthier or discreet patrons.
- Kitchen run by Mirren, specializing in exotic tapas-style small plates designed for social eating during performances.
- Cellar and brewhouse produce in-house beer, ales, wine, and experimental gin for signature cocktails; emphasis on craft and flavor.
- Private party quarters include individual bedrooms and a shared lounge, making the venue both home and operational headquarters.
- Guest rooms accommodate performers, travelers, and story NPCs, creating natural hooks for intrigue or conflict.
- Smugglers’ basement beneath the club enables covert movement of goods and secret meetings.
- Hidden tunnel network connects to the outside; partially unfinished and dangerous, including an experimental gas system that can serve as hazard or tactical tool.
- Front entrance controlled by Trevor the Troll, who serves as an intimidating but steady bouncer; his wife manages cleaning and maintenance.
- Back alley discreet entrance used for smugglers, performers, and sensitive arrivals.
- Performer dressing rooms store costumes, props, and provide prep space before shows.
- General storage areas hold linens, brewing supplies, and equipment.
- Staff includes Mirren (chef and cellar oversight), Trevor (security), Trevor’s wife (upkeep), and Snaggletooth with goblin kids acting as runners and street-level informants.
- Clientele intentionally mixed: new-money elites and lower middle-class revelers share the same space, creating refined chaos and social tension.
- Atmosphere defined by low light, candle and gas glow, rich colors, music shifting from soft trios to wild cabaret, layered scents of perfume, ale, spice, and sea air.
- Overall identity: glamorous surface entertainment masking a layered underworld of secrets, smuggling, espionage, and personal stakes.

## Session 15
The night began in celebration.

The Gilded Gutter opened its doors for the first time, and Port Reverie answered. Music filled the room, glasses clinked, and the bar thrumed with the energy of something new — something people wanted to believe in.

Dockhands and merchants mixed with nobles and performers, rivalries hidden behind polite smiles. The Gilded Gutter wasn’t just open — it was being judged.

Baron moved through the crowd with intent, making herself visible, composed, and impressive among the wealthier guests. Eyes lingered. Conversations paused. This place — and the people behind it — mattered.

Lady Seraphine Vance stood out immediately: well-dressed, gracious, and at ease, laughing easily as attention gathered around her.

As the night deepened, talk turned to indulgence. Rare perfumes. Stimulants. Excess. During one such exchange, Banjolina quietly lifted a small vial from a perfumer named Renn — and drank it. Whatever the consequences, they did not come immediately.

And the night was going well.

Zhik’ta and Captain Penelope ran the bar flawlessly, serving drinks with confidence and flair. Compliments flowed. In the kitchen, Mirren’s food emptied plates and won quiet praise — even the infamous “toilet hobo wine” became a talking point rather than a joke.

For a brief, shining stretch, the Gilded Gutter wasn’t just open. It was _working_.

Then Lady Seraphine raised her glass — and collapsed mid-toast. Wine shattered on the floor. The music stopped. Panic rippled through the room.

You barred the doors and sent for the Salt Watch. Captain Alaric arrived soon after and placed the venue under lockdown. Statements were taken. The room fell quiet, heavy with suspicion.

In the confusion, DR-55 noticed a halfling trying to slip away. She called herself Lila — a thief, not a killer. A quiet deal was struck, and she vanished through the old smuggling tunnels beneath the cellar.

When the night finally stilled, Lady Seraphine lay stricken, the Watch held the doors, and the Gilded Gutter’s grand opening had become the scene of a crime.

The celebration was over.
