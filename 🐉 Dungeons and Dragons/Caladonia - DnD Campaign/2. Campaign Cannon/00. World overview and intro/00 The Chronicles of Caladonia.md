In Caladonia, a fragile truce holds between the **Kingdom of Arathor** and the **Dominion of Drakoria** amidst economic stagnation and rising tension.

As you navigate this delicate peace, you will encounter a land shaped by the mystical substance **Aetherium**, where political intrigue, espionage, and the emergence of powerful religious sects blur the lines between faith and ambition—promising both salvation and peril in equal measure.

---

## The Hard Truths

### The End of an Eternal Conflict?
A tenuous truce is in place after decades of war between the Kingdom of Arathor and the Dominion of Drakoria. Until recently, the two factions were locked in a bitter struggle for dominance over the Caladonia Archipelago and the Dragonspine Mountains.

### The Scars of War
In the aftermath of a devastating war, economic stagnation grips the once-thriving coastal region. Refugee settlements strain resources, military presence remains heavy, environmental damage scars the land, and cultural tensions simmer beneath the surface.

As you traverse this fragile peace, you must navigate political intrigue, espionage, and the ever-present threat of renewed conflict.

### The Age of Aetherium
Aetherium is a mystical substance originating from the distant lands of Valoria. Introduced to Caladonia only within the last decade, it is rapidly spreading in influence.

Its wondrous properties fuel advanced technologies, though access remains tightly controlled. Many see Aetherium as a potential savior—capable of reigniting commerce and culture after years of war—while others fear its consequences.

### “Only the Gods Can Save Us…”
Long periods of instability have led to the rise of powerful religious sects. Increasingly, the population clings to dogma and blind faith over logic and reason.

## Factions of Caladonia

### Kingdom of Arathor
Led by **King Alistair Brightblade**, the Kingdom embodies ideals of honor and valor. Beneath this noble façade lies a web of political compromise and quiet intrigue.

Those who seek power must navigate the complexities of duty and morality in a realm where appearances are often deceiving.

### Dominion of Drakoria
Under the enigmatic rule of **Empress Sylvara Shadowheart**, Drakoria is shaped by dark magic and shifting allegiances.

Loyalty is currency, alliances are forged in shadow, and ambition carries lethal consequences. Survival depends on knowing which secrets to uncover—and which to leave buried.

### The Ironclad Company
A mercenary company backed by powerful merchant guilds, the Ironclad Company is a renowned band of sellswords operating across Caladonia and beyond.

They accept contracts from noble houses and desperate villages alike, caring little for banners so long as the coin is good.

### The Pirate Lords
The Pirate Lords of the Crimson Coast are feared and revered in equal measure. Commanding vast fleets and hidden coves, their infamous stronghold—**The Bloodharbour**—serves as a haven for scoundrels and buccaneers.

Despite the uneasy peace, tensions between the Pirate Lords and the militaries of Arathor and Drakoria continue to rise.

### Order of the Silver Sun
In the hallowed halls of the Order of the Silver Sun, devout seekers find solace beneath their deity’s benevolent gaze.

Yet beneath the veneer of piety lies a faction deeply entwined with politics and hidden agendas, where faith and ambition are often indistinguishable.

### Cult of the Black Moon
Under the moon’s eerie glow, the Cult of the Black Moon whispers of forbidden knowledge.

Through secretive rituals, its adherents confront the allure of esoteric magic and dangerous truths—risking revelation or damnation along the path of devotion.

## Your Characters

- Characters begin at **1st level**. This adventure is expected to reach **level 3**, with the option to expand into a broader campaign or West Marches-style play.
- You gain **one free feat** at level 1.
- Use **point-buy or standard array**.
- All **official D&D 5e material** is allowed, including optional rules and variants.
- Firearms, explosives, and steam-punk-adjacent equipment exist but are rare. Expect scrutiny and attention if used openly.
- The tone is **light-hearted, campy high fantasy**. Please leave edgelord characters at the door.
- If your name is **Mel**, you may be a dinosaur in a top hat pretending to be human.
- If your name is **Bruce**, you may wear bikini plate mail. This *will* become a problem later.

## House Rules

### Medic!
- Healing potions:
  - **Action**: heal for maximum value.
  - **Bonus action**: roll for healing.
- Re-roll natural 1s on healing spells (does not apply to potions).

### The Man in Brussels
- Known languages do **not** need to be declared until first use. Once declared, they are locked.
- Each character starts with **one additional language**.

### Inspire Me
If you have Inspiration, you may spend it in the following ways (in addition to the standard use):

- **Outta My Way!**  
  Move yourself to a higher position in the initiative order at the start of a round. This becomes your new permanent position.

- **Stay Hydrated**  
  Regain an expended feature or resource that normally resets on a short rest.

- **More DOTS!**  
  Regain one expended spell slot. Maximum slot level = spellcasting proficiency − 1.

- **Hey, Where’d You Get That Scar?**  
  When reduced to 0 HP, roll on the injury table.

- **I Know a Guy** *(Party-wide: once per session)*  
  Declare an NPC you know and briefly describe the relationship.  
  When you meet them, roll Charisma:
  - 1–4: Hostile  
  - 5–12: Neutral  
  - 13+: Helpful

- **Semper Paratus** *(Party-wide: once per session)*  
  Produce any mundane item (≤10 gp) that you could reasonably have purchased earlier. Gold is deducted immediately.

### Wanna Know How I Got These Scars?
When reduced to 0 HP, roll on the injury table.  
(No, you cannot see the injury table.)

### Back to Basics
Casters gain their flavor cantrip for free:
- Wizard / Sorcerer: *Prestidigitation*
- Druid / Ranger: *Druidcraft*
- Cleric / Warlock: *Thaumaturgy*

If you would already gain this cantrip, choose another cantrip from your class list instead.

## Welcome to the Bloodharbour

A haven for scoundrels and buccaneers, **you and your companions begin as slaves**, forced to fight in the Blood Arena beneath the Pirate stronghold known as **The Bloodharbour**.

For generations, the Greger family has profited by pitting slaves and beasts against one another for sport.

Will you survive the arena?  
Will you endure the brutal prison life?  
Will you escape the Bloodharbour?

## I Need More Lore!

Additional regions and factions, should you wish to use them:

### The Magocracy of Astrala
Located in the Valorian Plains, Astrala is defined by ley lines, arcane ruins, and the towering Arcane Spire of Arcanum.

Scholars, adventurers, and seekers of forbidden knowledge flock here from across the world.

### The Confederation of Free Cities
A chain of independent city-states along the Crossroads Coast, the Free Cities are cultural melting pots and commercial hubs.

From Port Haven’s markets to Arcanvale’s academies, opportunity abounds.

### The Order of the Silver Lance
Operating from fortified keeps along the Arathor–Drakoria border, the Silver Lance defends against monsters, wilderness threats, and raiders.

Honor and duty are upheld amid constant danger.

### The Dominion of Ironcliff
A militaristic society forged through conquest, Ironcliff controls the Ironspire Mountains and Bloodied Fields.

Expansion and dominance define its identity.

### The Union of the Fallen Leaf
Hidden within the Wildwood Enclave and Misty Moors, druidic circles safeguard ancient rites and natural balance.

Civilization’s advance is viewed with suspicion—and sometimes hostility.

### The League
Centered in the Merchant Princedoms and Gilded Cities, the League thrives on craftsmanship, innovation, and commerce.

From Port Prosperity to Skyreach, creativity and ambition rule.
