# Part I: World Powers

## The Kingdom of Arathor

**Motto:** _“Trade flows where trust endures.”_
**Alignment:** Lawful Neutral
**Seat:** The Arathorian Embassy, Old Coins

**Agenda:** Secure grain and textile trade dominance; protect Arathorian merchants abroad.
**Assets:** Well-funded embassy, merchant navy, network of diplomats and clerics.
**Methods:** Tariff negotiations, subsidized shipping, covert economic manipulation.
**Relations:** Friendly with the Syndicate of Coin, wary of Drakoria, disdains the Crimson Coast.

**Player Hooks:**

- Protect or sabotage a grain convoy that could tilt food prices across the continent.

- Retrieve a stolen diplomatic letter implicating Arathor in piracy.

- Escort a noble envoy seeking secret trade concessions from the Ironclads.


**Influence Actions:**

- Aid Arathorian traders (+1)

- Expose embassy corruption (-1)


**DM Note:** Arathor uses polite diplomacy to mask deep paranoia about losing dominance. They rarely act overtly—preferring to purchase stability with long-term contracts.

## The Dominion of Drakoria

**Motto:** _“Strength ensures peace.”_
**Alignment:** Lawful Evil
**Seat:** The Iron Embassy, Old Coins

**Agenda:** Expand naval access and secure dominance in shipbuilding and steel markets.
**Assets:** Skilled engineers, alchemical steelworks, private mercenary companies.
**Methods:** Coercive trade deals, blackmail, industrial espionage.
**Relations:** Strong alliance with the Ironclad Company; direct rival of Arathor.

**Player Hooks:**

- Infiltrate a Drakorian forge rumored to be constructing living ships.

- Smuggle or intercept weapon prototypes being tested in the harbor.

- Broker or sabotage a secret shipbuilding contract with the Ironclads.


**Influence Actions:**

- Provide stolen blueprints (+1)

- Leak naval movements (-1)


**DM Note:** Drakoria’s diplomats are all veterans—their negotiations feel like battles fought with ink instead of steel.

---

## The Ironclad Company

**Motto:** _“Peace through Strength, and Strength for Sale.”_
**Alignment:** Neutral Evil
**Seat:** The Ironclad Garrison, Crown Harbor

**Agenda:** Maintain contracts with Port Reverie and expand private security influence across the world.
**Assets:** 3,000 elite soldiers, ships-for-hire, mechanical war golems.
**Methods:** Protection rackets disguised as contracts, intimidation, privateering.
**Relations:** Allied with Drakoria, distrusted by the Neutrality Watch, loathed by the Workers’ Compact.

**Player Hooks:**

- Guard an Ironclad convoy—or rob it.

- Investigate missing Ironclad payrolls suspected stolen by rebels.

- Negotiate peace between Ironclad enforcers and dock unions.


**Influence Actions:**

- Accept Ironclad protection jobs (+1)

- Side with striking workers (-1)


**DM Note:** Every Ironclad officer believes they’re the only thing keeping the city from collapsing into chaos—and they might be right.

---

## The Crimson Coast Pirates

**Motto:** _“Freedom flies no flag.”_
**Alignment:** Chaotic Neutral
**Seat:** Hidden enclaves throughout Crown Harbor and The Sump

**Agenda:** Launder plunder, recruit new crews, undermine national navies.
**Assets:** Dozens of disguised ships, smuggling tunnels, bribed harbor masters.
**Methods:** Smuggling, blackmail, extortion, covert alliances.
**Relations:** Transactional friendship with the Syndicate of Coin; constant tension with the Saltwatch.

**Player Hooks:**

- Retrieve a cursed treasure that must not leave port.

- Track down a pirate crew using false trade marks.

- Protect (or betray) a smuggler turned informant.


**Influence Actions:**

- Help launder goods (+1)

- Turn over a pirate captain (-1)


**DM Note:** The Crimson Coast is less an organization than a rumor—alliances shift like tides. Their fleets appear united only when threatened.

---

## The Order of the Silver Sun

**Motto:** _“By Light, Truth Endures.”_
**Alignment:** Lawful Good
**Seat:** The Radiant Hall, Silkwater

**Agenda:** Promote moral conduct and justice through art, charity, and public ritual.
**Assets:** Churches, orphanages, propaganda theaters.
**Methods:** Public influence campaigns, sermons, performances, and donations.
**Relations:** Respectful toward the Concordat of Tides; covertly opposes the Cult of the Black Moon.

**Player Hooks:**

- Guard a sacred procession during a volatile festival.

- Investigate corruption within the Order’s own ranks.

- Protect a performer spreading controversial moral plays.


**Influence Actions:**

- Aid their charitable works (+1)

- Publicly discredit their sermons (-1)


**DM Note:** The Silver Sun wields faith as theater. They’re as likely to stage a play as a crusade.

---

## The Cult of the Black Moon

**Motto:** _“Every oath casts a shadow.”_
**Alignment:** Chaotic Evil
**Seat:** Secret shrines beneath The Sump

**Agenda:** Spread chaos through broken oaths and spiritual corruption.
**Assets:** Fanatical devotees, infiltrators within bureaucracies.
**Methods:** Assassinations, false contracts, and whispered temptations.
**Relations:** Eternal enmity with the Concordat of Tides; courted by criminal syndicates.

**Player Hooks:**

- Destroy a shrine disguised as a debtor’s guild.

- Rescue someone cursed by a broken oath.

- Uncover which faction secretly funds the cult.


**Influence Actions:**

- Protect their rituals (+1)

- Expose a cult cell (-1)


**DM Note:** Their influence is viral—they thrive wherever promises are broken. If the party betrays allies, the Black Moon notices.
