# 🕵️ Chapter 5: Factions

### _The Markets of Power_

> _"In Reverie, gold buys silence, favors buy loyalty, and secrets buy everything else."_

Port Reverie is a city where influence is the most valuable currency. Beneath the formal neutrality of the Wheel of Nine lies a constant negotiation between world powers, local factions, guilds, and syndicates. Each group trades favors, secrets, and strategic restraint in the pursuit of advantage. For Dungeon Masters, factions are the living veins of the city—they shape the flow of information, dictate opportunities, and react dynamically to player actions.

---

## Faction System: Influence as Currency

### Overview

Each faction tracks **Influence (0–4)** representing its attitude toward the party:

|Level|Standing|Example Attitude|
|---|---|---|
|**0**|Hostile|The faction views the party as enemies or saboteurs.|
|**1**|Indifferent|The faction knows of the party but doesn’t care.|
|**2**|Neutral|The faction sees the party as useful or unthreatening.|
|**3**|Favored|The faction provides aid, favors, or contracts.|
|**4**|Allied|The faction treats the party as trusted operatives or partners.|

Influence rises or falls through actions, downtime efforts, and story events. Favoring one faction usually costs Influence with its rivals.

### Downtime Actions (Examples)

|Action|Effect|
|---|---|
|**Broker Truce**|Attempt to mediate peace between rivals; if successful, gain +1 Influence with both.|
|**Expose Corruption**|Reveal scandalous truths; gain +1 Influence with one faction, lose -1 with another.|
|**Perform Favor**|Complete a minor quest for a faction (delivery, sabotage, protection). Gain +1 Influence.|
|**Sow Doubt**|Spread rumors or misinformation; reduce another faction’s Influence by 1.|
|**Invest Capital**|Spend 500 gp per tier to strengthen ties with mercantile factions.|
