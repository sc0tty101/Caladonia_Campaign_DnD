# 🧭 Chapter 1: Overview

> *"All oaths bind at the water's edge."*
> — Official Motto of Port Reverie

Perched on a vast crescent bay at the convergence of three major continental trade routes, Port Reverie stands as the world's undisputed center of international commerce and diplomacy. This sprawling metropolis houses nearly half a million souls from every corner of the known world, making it larger than many national capitals. Emissaries of a dozen kingdoms maintain permanent embassies here, while merchant princes command trading fleets from marble palaces overlooking the world's busiest harbor. The metropolis extends far beyond its ancient walls, sprawling across multiple harbors, terraced hillsides, and artificial islands connected by an intricate network of bridges and canals. What began as a pragmatic haven for neutral trade has evolved into a economic and political superpower that no kingdom can afford to ignore. Wars have been prevented by negotiations in Port Reverie's halls; trade routes spanning continents originate from its docks; and the city's banking houses finance kingdoms and merchant empires alike.

For adventurers, Reverie offers unparalleled opportunities—and dangers. The city's neutrality creates a unique environment where assassins toast paladins in the same taverns, where pirates negotiate with priests under lanternlight, and where a single contract signed in the right parlor can reshape the balance of nations.

## City at a Glance
**Population.** 340,000 within the city proper; ~85,000 in the greater metropolitan area including outer districts, satellite towns, and anchorages

**Government.** The Wheel of Nine (rotating First Spoke)

**Defense.** The Saltwatch (12,000 active guards); Harbor Fleet (40 warships); coastal fortifications; signal tower network; contracted Ironclad Company garrison (3,000 troops); letters-of-marque privateers in times of crisis

**Commerce.** Primary exports: Silks & luxury textiles; precision instruments & magical devices; banking & insurance services; rare spell components; specialized ships & naval technology. Major imports: Raw materials from six continents; exotic goods; bulk foodstuffs; precious metals; diplomatic intelligence

**Alignment.** Neutral (by treaty and temperament)

**Atmosphere.** Transactional yet theatrical; wit and wordcraft serve as currency alongside gold

## Other Names
Port Reverie is known by several epithets throughout the world:
- **The Nine-Spoked City** (referring to its government)
- **Salt-Crown** (a poetic reference to its position and power)
- **The Neutral Ground** (diplomatic circles)

Locals often simply call it "Reverie" or "the Port."

## Major Factions
The city's neutrality makes it a meeting ground for powers that would clash elsewhere. These groups maintain significant influence within Port Reverie:

### World Powers
**Kingdom of Arathor.** The western kingdom maintains an embassy in Old Coins and angles for favorable tariffs on their grain exports.

**Dominion of Drakoria.** Arathor's eastern rival keeps envoys in the city and seeks naval access through Reverie's waters.

**Ironclad Company.** This mercenary organization provides security contracts and seeks to expand their influence through purchased stability.

**Pirate Lords of the Crimson Coast.** Various captains launder plunder through intermediaries and recruitment networks.

**Order of the Silver Sun.** This lawful good organization shapes public sentiment through morality plays and charitable works.

**Cult of the Black Moon.** Shadow-worshippers gnaw at the city's foundations where the light of law grows thin.

### Local Powers
**The Marking House Consortium.** The organization that controls the official "mark" system—magical stamps and documentation required for all legitimate trade entering and leaving Port Reverie. This powerful guild of enchanters, bureaucrats, and customs officials can make or break any commercial venture through their authentication services.

**The Concordat of Tides.** A powerful inter-faith organization that has evolved far beyond its religious origins to become Port Reverie's unofficial moral authority. Originally formed to prevent religious conflicts between visiting pilgrims and merchants, the Concordat now mediates disputes, provides social services, and maintains the city's elaborate network of oaths and sacred bonds that underpin international commerce. They control marriage contracts, witness major treaties, and their priests serve as trusted neutral parties in sensitive negotiations. Most importantly, they maintain the "Sacred Ledger"—a mystical record of all oaths sworn in the city that somehow compels adherence to agreements made under their auspices.

**The Syndicate of Coin.** An alliance of banking houses, currency exchangers, and financial institutions that effectively controls Port Reverie's monetary policy. They set exchange rates, approve major loans, and can freeze assets across multiple kingdoms.

**The Neutrality Watch.** An elite organization of investigators, mediators, and enforcement specialists dedicated to maintaining Port Reverie's neutral status. They monitor treaty violations, investigate diplomatic incidents, and have authority to detain anyone threatening the city's political balance.

**The Cultural Preservation Society.** What appears to be an academic organization focused on arts and festivals actually serves as a sophisticated intelligence network. They use cultural events and artistic exchanges to gather information and influence public opinion across international boundaries.

**The Workers' Compact.** A grassroots confederation representing laborers across all industries—from dock stevedores to theater stagehands, from warehouse workers to canal maintenance crews. Born from necessity when foreign merchant houses began exploiting local workers, the Compact has evolved into a powerful political force that can shut down entire districts through coordinated action. Unlike traditional unions, they practice mutual aid, collective bargaining, and community self-defense across craft lines.
