# 🕰️ Chapter 2: History

Port Reverie's past is a tapestry woven from pragmatism, opportunism, and the occasional stroke of brilliant diplomacy. Unlike many cities that grew from a single founding vision, Reverie evolved through necessity—becoming neutral not from idealism, but from survival.

## Founding Myths

Three competing legends attempt to explain Port Reverie's origins. Like most mythology, each contains elements of truth wrapped in maritime superstition.

### The Flotsam Fleet
*Most popular among sailors and dock workers*

According to this tale, a mismatched convoy of refugee ships and rogue vessels was driven by an unnatural storm onto a sandbar in the crescent bay. With their ships broken but their spirits intact, these castaways built the first docks from the timber of their ruined keels. The storm, they claimed, had been sent by sea-spirits to gather worthy souls in one place.

**Truth:** Archaeological evidence does suggest the earliest harbor was built using ship timber, and the bay's unique shape would naturally collect storm-driven vessels.

### The Sea-Goddess Compact
*Favored by temple priests and mystics*

This myth speaks of an ancient pact with a sea-goddess, promising calm waters in exchange for regular tithes and the maintenance of neutrality. Believers point to the mysterious chamber beneath Ninefold Hall as evidence of a forgotten temple, and note that the city's greatest storms coincide with broken oaths or treaty violations.

**Truth:** The chamber beneath Ninefold Hall is real, though its original purpose remains unclear. The city does experience unusual weather patterns during diplomatic crises.

### The Seven Timber Pact
*Preserved in official records and noble genealogies*

Seven ship captains, each representing different crews and interests, are said to have laid a plank from their flagships into the foundation of the first council hall. This created a symbolic bond requiring shared rule and unanimous consent for major decisions. Only one family name from the original seven—Saltmere—still appears on the Wheel of Nine.

**Truth:** Early governance documents do reference seven founding families, and the Saltmere line can trace their ancestry to Reverie's earliest days.

## The True History

Beneath the myths lies a simpler truth: geography and opportunity. The crescent bay lay perfectly positioned along emerging trade routes between the Kingdom of Arathor and the eastern markets beyond Drakoria. Early settlers were practical folk—privateers, traders, and refugees who recognized that neutrality offered better profits than picking sides.

The settlement grew rapidly as merchants discovered they could conduct business here without the political entanglements that plagued other ports. Within fifty years, Port Reverie had become an essential hub where rivals could meet on relatively neutral ground.

The city's founders weren't idealists; they were survivors who realized they were worth more alive and independent than conquered and controlled.

## Timeline of Major Events

### Year 0: The Reverie Accords
The treaty that established Port Reverie's official neutrality, safe harbor provisions, and the founding of the Wheel of Nine government. Representatives from seven major powers signed the original document, though the signatories have changed over the centuries.

*Key Provisions:*
- Guaranteed neutrality in external conflicts
- Safe harbor for all vessels (with limitations)
- Establishment of the rotating council system
- Legal framework for dispute resolution
- Trade protections and standardized currency exchange

### Year 43: The Merchant's Rebellion
Wealthy traders attempted to overthrow the Wheel system in favor of a plutocracy. The rebellion failed when dock workers and ship captains united against them, but it led to formal recognition of labor unions and the addition of the Commons spoke to the Wheel.

### Year 107: The Two-Tide War
Arathor and Drakoria's naval forces clashed directly in Reverie's bay, forcing the city to activate its harbor chains for the first time. The battle ended in stalemate, but the conflict led to amendments allowing private armed escorts and the establishment of the Saltwatch as a proper military force.

### Years 152–181: The Mercantile Bloom
A golden age of prosperity as silk, spice, and rare timber trade swelled the city's coffers. Grand theaters were built, elaborate festivals became annual traditions, and Port Reverie's cultural influence spread far beyond its waters. However, this wealth also attracted corruption and the first organized crime syndicates.

### Year 194: The Silent Fleet Incident
A plague ship carrying a mysterious cargo was denied entry to the harbor. When it sank in the bay, strange lights were seen beneath the water for months afterward. Official records were sealed, and several councilors resigned without explanation.

### Year 237: The Artificer Wars
Competing gnomish inventors turned the Craft district into a battleground of mechanical contraptions and alchemical explosions. The conflict ended with strict regulations on experimental magic and the establishment of designated testing areas outside the city walls.

## Recent Events

### The Shadow Wharf Riots (8 years ago)
Dockworkers revolted against Ironclad Company monopolies on cargo handling and warehouse access. The riots lasted three days and resulted in significant damage to the waterfront. Order was restored not through force, but through negotiation—the city expanded Ironclad's contracts while simultaneously lowering tariffs and improving worker conditions.

**Current Impact:** The Ironclad Company now maintains a permanent garrison in the city, but dock unions have gained considerable political power.

### The Silent Season (3 years ago)
A coordinated series of assassinations targeted envoys, merchant princes, and treaty negotiators over the course of six months. The killings were precise and professional, leaving no witnesses or evidence. Diplomatic activity ground to a halt as foreign powers withdrew their representatives.

The murders stopped as suddenly as they began, and normal relations resumed within a year. Rumors persist of a blood-sealed truce negotiated in the shadows between previously unknown parties.

**Current Impact:** Security for high-profile visitors has increased dramatically, and several new "consulting" firms offering protection services have established offices in the city.

### Aetherium Murmurs (ongoing)
Whispers speak of a lustrous, magic-hungry metal arriving in discrete shipments through back-channel traders. City officials publicly dismiss these rumors, but certain factions are reportedly paying enormous sums for even small quantities of the mysterious substance.

Few know the name "aetherium," and fewer dare speak it aloud. Those who ask too many questions have a tendency to quietly leave the city or stop asking questions altogether.

**Current Impact:** A shadow market has emerged for rare magical materials, and several established merchants have become unexpectedly wealthy through unknown means.
