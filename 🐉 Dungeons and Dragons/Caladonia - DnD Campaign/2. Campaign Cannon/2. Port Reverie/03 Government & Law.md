# ⚖️ Chapter 3: Government & Law

Port Reverie's government balances the need for effective leadership with its foundational principle of neutrality. The Wheel of Nine ensures that no single interest—whether commercial, military, or spiritual—can dominate the city's decisions.

## The Wheel of Nine

The city operates through nine specialized councils, each representing a crucial aspect of Port Reverie's governance. These "spokes" rotate the position of First Spoke monthly, with that councilor serving as the city's effective leader.

### The First Spoke
Each month, leadership rotates to the next spoke in sequence. The First Spoke has the authority to:
- Call emergency sessions
- Cast tie-breaking votes
- Represent the city in formal ceremonies
- Coordinate with the Saltwatch commander
- Sign treaties and trade agreements (with council approval)

This rotation ensures that no faction gains permanent control while maintaining effective day-to-day governance.

### Voting and Procedures
Standard decisions require a simple majority (5 of 9 votes). Constitutional changes, treaty modifications, and declarations of emergency require a supermajority (7 of 9 votes). The First Spoke votes normally but may cast an additional tie-breaking vote if needed.

Council sessions are public, though formal debates often resemble theatrical performances more than dry political proceedings. The citizenry expects eloquence and wit from their representatives.

## Ninefold Hall

The seat of government occupies a prominent position in the city center. The Council Chamber centers on a great oak wheel inlaid with brass, with nine seats arranged around its circumference. The wheel can be rotated to place any spoke in the "first" position—literally and symbolically.

Public galleries surround the chamber, accommodating up to 300 observers. Important votes often draw crowds who applaud or gasp at dramatic moments. The famous Bell of Binding hangs above the wheel; when rung, it signifies that a matter is formally closed and cannot be reopened for a full year.

**Notable Features:**
- The **Vault of Accords**: Secure chamber containing original treaties and founding documents
- **The Neutral Garden**: Courtyard where foreign dignitaries can meet informally
- **The Archive**: Public records and legal precedents available to citizens and visitors
- **The Whisper Gallery**: Acoustic anomaly that allows conversations to be overheard from specific locations

## The Saltwatch

Port Reverie's military force serves as city guard, harbor patrol, and diplomatic security. Unlike traditional armies, the Saltwatch prioritizes de-escalation and law enforcement over conquest.

**Organization:**
- **Watch Commander**: Reports directly to the First Spoke
- **Harbor Guard**: Patrols the docks and inspects incoming vessels
- **City Watch**: Maintains order in the districts
- **Treaty Guard**: Protects foreign dignitaries and embassy districts
- **Signal Corps**: Operates the lighthouse and warning towers

**Equipment:** Saltwatch members wear sea-blue uniforms with brass buttons and carry truncheons, crossbows, and curved sabres. Officers bear silver badges shaped like lighthouse beacons.

**Numbers:** Approximately 12,000 active members across multiple divisions, with another 8,000 reserves who can be called up during emergencies. This makes the Saltwatch larger than the standing armies of some small kingdoms.

## Laws and Legal System

Port Reverie's legal code balances the need for order with the practical realities of being a neutral haven for diverse peoples and interests.

### Core Legal Principles

**The Accords Immunity**
Registered envoys, diplomatic couriers, and officially recognized merchant crews enjoy limited immunity while in port. This protection can be revoked for crimes committed within city limits, but the process requires formal hearings and diplomatic notification.

**Weapons and Dueling**
- Open carry is permitted in most districts
- Weapons must be peace-bound or checked in embassy quarters, Highmarket Heights, and Ninefold Hall
- Dueling is legal if properly registered with seconds, agreed rules, and conducted in sanctioned arenas
- Killing during an illegal duel is prosecuted as murder

**Spellcraft Etiquette**
- Public spellcasting must be announced audibly before activation
- Covert enchantment of any citizen constitutes magical assault
- Divination magic used for commercial espionage is a civil offense
- Necromancy and charm effects are heavily regulated

**Maritime Law**
- Harbor Master has absolute authority over vessel movements
- Salvage rights follow traditional maritime custom
- Piracy charges require evidence of attacks within a 50-mile radius
- Privateering is legal with proper letters of marque

### Court System

**High Court**
Located in Ninefold Hall, the High Court handles serious crimes, treaty disputes, and appeals. Trials are public spectacles featuring advocate-performers who present evidence through dramatic presentations. Verdicts are delivered by a panel of three judges to audience applause or gasps.

**District Magistrates**
Each district maintains a chamber for civil disputes and minor crimes. Magistrates are appointed by the relevant spoke of the Wheel and confirmed by council vote. They handle:
- Contract disputes
- Property crimes under 100 gold pieces
- Public disturbances
- Licensing violations
- Immigration processing

**Commercial Arbitration**
The Merchant Syndicates operate private arbitration services for trade disputes. While not legally binding, their decisions are generally respected and enforced through economic pressure.

### Enforcement

The Saltwatch handles arrests and investigations, though many disputes are "resolved" through faction intermediaries before reaching official attention. The city's neutral status means that enforcement often focuses on maintaining public order rather than pursuing absolute justice.

**Punishments:**
- **Fines**: Most common penalty, often including restitution
- **Public Service**: Labor on civic projects or harbor maintenance
- **Banishment**: Temporary or permanent expulsion from the city
- **Imprisonment**: Reserved for serious crimes; the city jail holds up to 50 prisoners
- **Execution**: Rare, reserved for treason, murder, or treaty violations

## Corruption and Loopholes

Port Reverie's legal system, while functional, contains numerous opportunities for those willing to exploit technicalities and social connections.

**Common Exploits:**
- **Temporary Envoy Papers**: Fraudulent diplomatic credentials grant months of immunity
- **Contrived Duels**: Manufactured insults justify lethal "honor" combat
- **Linguistic Loopholes**: Spell announcements whispered in obscure languages
- **Jurisdictional Confusion**: Crimes committed on ships in harbor fall into legal gray areas
- **Purchase of Justice**: While illegal, fines and sentences often correlate with the defendant's ability to pay

The city's merchants and politicians generally view these corruption as the cost of doing business in a neutral port. As long as the exploitation doesn't threaten the city's stability or neutrality, authorities often turn a blind eye.

> *"In Reverie, the law is a ledger balanced by gold and nerve."*
> — Common saying among the city's legal professionals
