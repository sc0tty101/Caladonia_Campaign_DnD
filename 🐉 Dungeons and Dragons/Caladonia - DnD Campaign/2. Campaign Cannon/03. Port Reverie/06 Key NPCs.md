# 👥 Chapter 6: Key NPCs

Port Reverie thrives on the ambitions, schemes, and personalities of its diverse inhabitants. From political power brokers to street-level fixers, these individuals shape the daily life of the city and provide adventure opportunities for visiting heroes.

## Calvira Duskveil
*Half-Elf Patron of the Arts*

> *"Applause fades. Power lingers. I teach my performers to seek the second."*

**Appearance:** Statuesque half-elf with midnight hair streaked silver and bronze eyes that seem to catalog everything they see. She dresses in theatrical finery with discrete armor pieces worked into the design. Every movement appears deliberate and practiced, as if she's always performing for an invisible audience.

**Background:** Born to a minor noble family that lost their fortune in shipping disasters, Calvira learned early that reputation and connections matter more than bloodline. She rebuilt her family's influence through careful cultivation of the arts community and strategic alliances with rising political figures.

### Personality
**Public Persona:** A cultured patroness dedicated to elevating Pennycross and restoring its former glory as the city's premier entertainment district. She speaks eloquently about art's power to unite people across cultural and political divides.

**Private Truth:** A calculating power broker who views artists as investments and cultural events as opportunities to forge political alliances. She genuinely appreciates artistic talent but sees it primarily as a tool for influence.

**Speech Patterns:** Uses theatrical metaphors in conversation; speaks with a velvety voice that can shift from warm encouragement to cold dismissal. Never raises her voice—others quiet down to hear her.

### Goals and Motivations
- **Restore Pennycross:** Elevate the district's reputation without allowing any single faction to dominate it
- **Build a Cultural Empire:** Use artistic patronage to create a network of influence across multiple districts
- **Secure a Legacy:** Be remembered as the savior of Port Reverie's cultural life rather than just another forgotten noble

### Resources and Connections
**The Duskveil Box:** A luxurious private balcony in the Gilded Gutter that she rents to wealthy patrons for discrete meetings and negotiations.

**Political Influence:** Maintains relationships with sympathetic council members, particularly those on the Faiths, Commons, and Civic Works spokes.

**Information Network:** Uses her connections in the theater community to gather gossip and intelligence from across the city.

**Financial Resources:** While not enormously wealthy, she has enough capital to fund productions and purchase influence when necessary.

### Quirks and Habits
- Never enters a theater through the front doors—always uses stage entrances or private boxes
- Keeps a velvet pouch containing ticket stubs from every major performance she's produced
- Has an inexplicable hatred of pigeons, calling them "born critics with no appreciation for effort"
- Writes reviews of performances using different pseudonyms for various broadsheets

### Adventure Hooks
- **Information Brokerage:** Provides valuable intelligence about threats, opportunities, and political developments across the city
- **Network Access:** Can arrange introductions to contacts throughout Pennycross's mystical underworld
- **Resource Conflicts:** Her commitment to information neutrality may clash with party members who want her to take sides in conflicts

## Finnis "Finn" Greybough
*Halfling Fixer and Information Broker*

> *"I can get you in the door, mates — but getting you out again is your own affair."*

**Appearance:** A late-thirties halfling with sandy hair, quick eyes, and an easy smile. He wears patched but clean coats with numerous pockets, and always smells faintly of pipe tobacco. His handshake is firm and overly familiar—he treats everyone like an old friend within minutes of meeting them.

**Background:** Born in Pennycross to a family of street performers, Finn learned early how to read crowds and make connections. When his parents retired, he transitioned from performance to logistics, discovering a talent for knowing who needed what and who could provide it.

### Personality
**Public Face:** Warm, gregarious, and helpful. Finn genuinely likes people and enjoys solving their problems, though he expects fair compensation for his services.

**Hidden Depths:** More observant and calculating than his friendly demeanor suggests. He remembers every favor, debt, and slight, though he rarely acts on grudges immediately.

**Speech Patterns:** Uses "mate," "friend," and "pal" liberally; quotes odd snippets from broadsheets and handbills he's collected; has a tendency to ramble when nervous.

### Goals and Motivations
- **Maintain Independence:** Keep freelance performers and small businesses from being absorbed by larger guilds or criminal organizations
- **Profitable Partnerships:** Attach himself to rising stars and successful ventures for mutual benefit
- **Personal Security:** Quietly pay down a gambling debt that's grown uncomfortably large

### Resources and Connections
**Venue Relationships:** Knows the owners, managers, and staff of most entertainment venues in Pennycross and several other districts.

**Street Network:** Street vendors, carriage drivers, and day laborers serve as his informal information and courier network.

**Family Connections:** A cousin in the Saltwatch provides tips about patrol routes and planned raids.

**Supplier Contacts:** Can procure talent, props, costumes, and specialized equipment with reasonable notice.

### Quirks and Habits
- Collects broadsheets, handbills, and promotional materials—his room is wallpapered with them
- Claims to have won a drinking contest against a devil but becomes evasive when pressed for details
- Talks to his battered leather satchel, which he's named "Prudence"
- Has an excellent memory for names and faces but terrible memory for directions

### Adventure Hooks
- **Local Guide:** Provides essential information about Pennycross's mystical underworld, vendors, and unwritten rules
- **Leverage Target:** His accumulated debts make him valuable as pressure against the party or their allies
- **Information Conduit:** Trades overheard secrets and district gossip without always understanding their true significance or danger

## Major Antagonists

### Harry & Harold "The Velvet Knives"
*Gnome Serial Killers and Luxury Furriers*

**Public Face:** Harry and Harold present themselves as cultured gnome "gentlemen traders" who operate The Velvet Pelt, an upscale furrier shop catering to wealthy clientele. They host refined salons where they discuss literature and philosophy while displaying their finest wares. Both brothers measure potential clients with the same careful attention a tailor might give to expensive cloth.

**Physical Description:** Two gnomes of remarkably similar appearance despite having no blood relation—a coincidence that adds to their unsettling presence. Both appear to be in their apparent middle age, always impeccably dressed in coordinating (though not identical) outfits. They move with the same precise, deliberate gestures and speak in similarly soft, cultured voices. Their hands are always perfectly manicured—a detail that becomes more sinister upon closer acquaintance.

### The Hidden Truth
Behind their respectable facade, Harry and Harold are methodical serial killers driven by an obsession with perfect texture and absolute control. They maintain a secret vault containing preserved human skins, each tagged with an affectionate brass nameplate bearing details about the "donor."

**Their Process:**
1. **Selection:** They choose victims based on skin quality, focusing on those with fine complexions and interesting markings or scars
2. **Seduction:** Victims are lured through social events, exclusive fittings, or promises of lucrative business deals
3. **Harvesting:** Victims are carefully sedated and skinned with surgical precision in a hidden workshop
4. **Preservation:** The skins are treated with alchemical processes and stored in climate-controlled conditions
5. **Disposal:** Bodies are disposed of through contacts in the shipping industry

**Luxury Commissions:** Their most exclusive clients can commission garments lined or trimmed with human leather, though these pieces are never sold publicly and are offered only to select individuals who share their particular tastes.

### Warning Signs and Tells
Observant characters might notice these disturbing behaviors:
- Handle furs with lingering, possessive strokes while describing their "provenance"
- Compliments focus intensely on skin quality, hair texture, and "the lines of the face"
- Insist on conducting fittings with bare hands, letting touches linger uncomfortably long
- A sweet-sour tannery scent clings to their shop despite extensive perfuming
- A handle-less wardrobe in Harold's private office that seems oddly out of place

### Motivations
The brothers are driven by a combination of aesthetic obsession and psychological need for control. They view their victims not as people but as sources of beautiful materials, and they take genuine pride in their "craftsmanship." Their polite, cultured demeanor masks a complete lack of empathy for human suffering.

### Long-term Plans
1. **Recovery:** Diplomatically reclaim property stolen from them through lavish hospitality and barely veiled threats
2. **Sabotage:** Undermine competitors and enemies through social manipulation and carefully orchestrated "accidents"
3. **The Hunt:** Begin stalking new victims, sending them personalized "gifts" that serve as both threat and promise

### Playing the Velvet Knives
These antagonists work best as slow-burn villains whose true nature is revealed gradually. Start with social encounters where they seem merely eccentric, then escalate through increasingly uncomfortable interactions until their true horror becomes apparent.

**Tone:** Affable, patient, and frighteningly deliberate—they treat murder as just another business transaction requiring proper etiquette and attention to detail.
